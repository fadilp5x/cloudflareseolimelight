About The Limelight

    The Limelight is a bimonthly academic web magazine established under Shamsul Huda Islamic Academy, Kerala. It serves as a platform for critical inquiry, creative reflection, and interdisciplinary engagement at the intersection of culture, scholarship, and faith. The publication seeks to illuminate ideas often overlooked in mainstream discourse, bringing together voices from literature, anthropology, history, and theology to foster a deeper understanding of the intellectual and spiritual life of Muslim societies and beyond.

    Our Mission

    Our mission is to cultivate a culture of thoughtful scholarship and creative exchange. Rooted in both academic integrity and spiritual insight, The Limelight aims to bridge classical traditions of knowledge with contemporary perspectives. By engaging scholars, students, and readers in dialogue across disciplines, the magazine aspires to build a living archive of ideas that reflect the diversity, depth, and dynamism of Islamic intellectual life.

    Our Vision

    We envision The Limelight as an evolving space where the pursuit of knowledge transcends boundaries of geography, language, and discipline. The magazine seeks to nurture a new generation of thinkers capable of engaging critically with modernity while remaining grounded in ethical and spiritual consciousness. Through consistent inquiry and collaboration, The Limelight hopes to contribute to a global conversation on knowledge, culture, and community.

    Editorial Disclaimer

    The content published in The Limelight represents the perspectives of individual authors and contributors. While we encourage intellectual freedom and informed critique, the opinions expressed in articles do not necessarily reflect those of the editorial board or its parent institution. Articles may be shared or republished for non-commercial purposes with due citation and prior permission from the editorial team.
